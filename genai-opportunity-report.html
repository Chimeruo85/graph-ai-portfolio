Strategic Advisory: Generative AI Opportunities

Modernizing the Claims Management System for the National Health Insurance Authority (NHIA), Nigeria

Executive Summary

Under the mandates of the NHIS Act (2005) and the reformed NHIA Act (2022), the National Health Insurance Authority of Nigeria is strategically poised to scale universal health coverage for all Nigerians. Achieving this objective requires streamlining administrative systems, especially the Claims Management System. Currently, claims processing and verification remain heavily resource-intensive, which can lead to delayed payments to healthcare providers and increased administrative overhead.

This report evaluates concrete, secure, and highly feasible opportunities to integrate Generative Artificial Intelligence (GenAI) and automation into NHIA's claims architecture. By utilizing secure Large Language Models (LLMs) to ingest, evaluate, and reconcile unstructured medical bills, clinical narratives, and compliance manuals, NHIA can significantly reduce processing times, minimize errors, and detect fraudulent activities before payouts occur.

NHIA Overview & Strategic Mandate

The National Health Insurance Authority (NHIA) of Nigeria, managing a dedicated team of 501–1,000 employees, serves as the regulatory and operational vehicle ensuring financial access to quality healthcare. To fulfill this public mission, NHIA handles complex data networks consisting of private and public healthcare facilities, HMOs, and individual beneficiaries. Modernizing claims ingestion not only drives operational efficiency internally but acts as a key catalyst in improving overall healthcare quality, boosting provider satisfaction, and ensuring national scale.

Top Generative AI & Automation Opportunities

High Feasibility

1. Intelligent Medical Coding & ICD-10 Parsing

Healthcare providers across Nigeria submit medical claims with highly varied structures, handwriting transcriptions, and varying levels of adherence to standard ICD-10 classifications. GenAI models can instantly read unstructured text, identify diagnoses, procedures, and prescriptions from the clinic notes, and translate or match them to standard NHIA-approved clinical guidelines and billing codes.

Business Impact Reduces manual claims auditing cycles by up to 60%, drastically minimizing human transcription and categorization errors.
Suggested First Step Create a pilot project using an open-source LLM hosted locally to ingest 1,000 historical anonymized billing receipts and assess parsing accuracy against human standards.
High Impact

2. Autonomous Policy Adjudication Advisor

Ensuring that claim items align directly with benefit packages defined under the NHIA Act 2022 is a complex task. By applying Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), NHIA can create an internal "policy agent" that automatically cross-checks medical claims against active national policy manuals, immediately flagging unsupported procedures and highlighting the precise legal/policy citations.

Business Impact Ensures total legal and operational compliance during claims reviews, preserving valuable health coverage funds for valid claims.
Suggested First Step Upload current NHIA benefits manuals and drug formularies to a secure Vector Database to build a question-answering prototype for claim adjusters.
Fraud Prevention

3. GenAI-Powered Waste & Abuse Detection

Traditional rule-based fraud detection struggles to spot nuanced patterns of waste, upcoding, or duplicate billing hidden within complex narrative files. GenAI excels at detecting anomalous narrative semantics across historical claim batches, highlighting duplicate treatments disguised under different clinical phrasing.

Business Impact Saves up to 5-15% of annual claims expenditure by identifying leakage, unbundled billing, and fraud prior to payment release.
Suggested First Step Establish a structured loop where flagged outliers are fed back into the training loop, refining the AI detection criteria continuously.
Provider Experience

4. Multilingual Claims Support & Feedback Portal

Providers often experience communication gaps regarding rejected or partially paid claims. A GenAI-driven conversational agent can sit on the provider portal, helping clinics understand exact rejection reasons, providing guidance on how to fix formatting errors, and supporting inquiries in English, Pidgin, Hausa, Yoruba, and Igbo.

Business Impact Decreases support tickets and call-center volume by 40%, cultivating stronger trust and partnerships between the NHIA and healthcare providers.
Suggested First Step Develop a secure chat interface connected to the claims engine API, allowing providers to securely request real-time status updates.

Implementation Horizon

To introduce Generative AI safely within a federal agency handling sensitive citizen data, we recommend a tiered implementation schedule:

Phase Timeline Focus Areas Risk Level
Phase 1: Foundations Months 1–3 RAG setup on public manuals; data privacy framework development; provider portal assistance. Low
Phase 2: Claims Co-Pilot Months 4–8 Intelligent ICD-10 Parsing; assistive human-in-the-loop review tool for claim adjusters. Medium
Phase 3: Automated Trust Months 9–12 Advanced Fraud, Waste, and Abuse (FWA) detection pipelines integrated directly into the core ERP. Medium

Conclusion

By leveraging Generative AI, the National Health Insurance Authority of Nigeria can transition its Claims Management System from a highly manual, administrative bottleneck into a streamlined, automated, and secure asset. This digital evolution directly supports the NHIA Act’s vision: delivering financial access to quality healthcare for all Nigerians rapidly, reliably, and with absolute accountability.
